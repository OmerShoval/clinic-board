Group Themes — patterns across the cohort

What's emerging in Day 4

Trial counts reveal the truth

Koby: 1/3 cutbacks fully correct. Tali: 3/5 hand openings. Roee: 10/10 foot position. Counting attempts + good attempts gives a success rate that wave count alone can't show.

Foot position is the universal blocker

Koby, Roee, and Michal are all actively working on back foot placement and astrodek awareness. Three different stages — but the same root cause: feet not in the right place to generate power.

Hands as a unit

Tali and Jonathan are both working on the "Pilates ball" concept — two hands moving together, not independently. This is a body-coordination unlock that once it clicks, carries over to everything.

Speed + readiness = the timing window

Koby's cutback (speed first, then move), Michal's foot shift (only when on wave with speed), Roee's hand stop (wait for the right moment). Timing is the unifying lesson this day.

Minimal, intentional movement beats aggression

Michal: small quick foot shift beats big lift. Tali: board "dances" when too aggressive. Roee: gradual hand = stable legs. The refinement phase is kicking in across the cohort.

Mental state as a performance variable

Tali explored zone states and pre-session rituals (morning meditation, energy check). Jonathan noticed that over-focusing on one thing makes him forget another. The mind-body loop is becoming a shared topic.
